Our Story

Built Beside
the Ancient Lake

"We didn't build a hotel. We built a home ‚Äî and then opened the door."

G.M.T. Sorabora began as a dream held by one family: to share the quiet majesty of Sorabora Wewa with travellers willing to slow down and truly see Sri Lanka. Not the curated, hurried Sri Lanka of tourist trails ‚Äî but the real one. The one that moves at the pace of the water.

The villa was carefully designed to sit lightly on the land. Local craftsmen shaped every beam and tile. Gardens were planted with native species that the land already knew. The kitchen sources from farmers in the surrounding villages of Mahiyanganaya.

Sorabora Wewa ‚Äî the lake you see from every room ‚Äî is no ordinary reservoir. According to legend, it was built by Dunu, a brilliant engineer-slave, thousands of years ago without the knowledge of writing or formal tools. It still irrigates the land today. We carry that spirit of honest, purposeful craft in everything we do.

Our guests are not guests so much as they are house friends ‚Äî people who come for a night and leave with a piece of the lake inside them. We measure our success not in occupancy rates, but in the number of people who return.
